How to Win Friends & Influence People was a classic book written by Dale Carnegie. I want to share my notes that I wrote while reading the book.

He separated the book into four parts:
1. Tips to deal with people.
2. Tips to make people like you.
3. Tips to get people to agree with your way of thinking.
4. Tips to be a good leader.

## 1. Tips to deal with people
Be sincere in your support and generous in your praise.

1. Avoid blame and criticism. Do not judge, and they don't judge you. Don't criticize them, because we'll do the same thing if we're in the same situation. Any idiot can criticize and complain, but needs character to understand and forgive. Instead of being angry with them, try to understand why they do that.
2. Give honest appreciation. People want to feel important and needed. Never miss an opportunity to give genuine appreciation.
3. Raise the great desire in them. The best way to someone to do something is to show them how it benefits them too.

## 2. Tips to make people like you
We can make more friends in a short time by genuinely caring about others, rather than trying to get people interested in us.

1. Give genuine attention to others.
2. Make a smile.
3. Remember their name.
4. Be a good listener. Encourage them to talk more about themselves.
5. Speak according to their interests.
6. Try to make the other person feel important.

## 3. Tips to get people to agree with your way of thinking

Arguments rarely produce true winners. People are more likely to change when they feel understood, not attacked.
1. Avoid pointless arguments.
2. Respect others' opinions and avoid saying, "You're wrong".
3. If you're wrong, admit it quickly and sympathetically.
4. Start in a friendly manner.
5. Get the other person to say "yes" from the start.
6. Encourage them to speak more.
7. Let the ideas feel like they're coming from them.
8. Try to see things from the other person's perspective.
9. Be sympathetic to others' ideas and desires.
10. Encourage them with noble motives.
11. Make ideas vivid and understandable.
12. Provide stimulating challenges.

## 4. Tips to be a good leader

Begin with sincere praise and appreciation. This helps the other person feel valued. Then mistakes can be corrected in a way that does not hurt their confidence.
1.	Start with sincere praise and appreciation.
2.	Point out mistakes in a gentle and indirect way.
3.	Admit your own mistakes before correcting others.
4.	Ask questions instead of giving orders.
5.	Help others keep their dignity and not feel embarrassed.
6.	Praise every improvement, even small progress.
7.	Speak positively so others feel trusted and capable.
8.	Encourage them and make mistakes feel easier to fix.
9.	Help others feel happy and willing to do what we suggest.
